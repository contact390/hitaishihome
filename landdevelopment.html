Land Development FAQs

What's the difference between raw land and entitled land? +

Raw land has no approvals for development, while entitled land has gone through the zoning and approval process to allow specific uses and densities. Entitled land typically commands a significant premium (2-5x) over raw land prices due to reduced risk and shorter development timelines.

How long does the land entitlement process typically take? +

The entitlement process can range from 6 months for simple zoning verifications to 3+ years for complex master-planned communities. Most suburban projects take 12-24 months from initial application to final approval, depending on jurisdiction and project complexity.

What are the key factors that determine land value? +

Key valuation factors include: location and proximity to amenities, zoning and development potential, availability of utilities, topography and buildability, market demand for proposed uses, and comparable land sales in the area. Future growth projections also significantly impact value.

What are the advantages of investing in land versus developed properties? +

Land investments offer higher appreciation potential, lower maintenance costs, and flexibility in development timing. They can also provide tax advantages through 1031 exchanges and often have lower property taxes. However, they typically don't generate income until developed.

How do you identify land with strong appreciation potential? +

We look for parcels in the path of growth, near planned infrastructure improvements, with zoning flexibility, and in markets with strong demographic trends. Other indicators include increasing land prices in adjacent areas, new employer announcements, and transportation corridor expansions.
