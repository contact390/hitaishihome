Legal & Documentation

Ensuring compliant agricultural land transactions

Essential Documents

  • 7/12 Extract & Mutation Entries
  • Title Deed (Minimum 30-year chain)
  • Encumbrance Certificate
  • Soil Health Card (Recommended)
  • Water Rights Documentation
  • Land Conversion Certificate (If applicable)
  • Survey Map & Boundaries

State-Specific Regulations

Maharashtra

Agricultural land can be purchased by non-farmers up to 8 acres. Special permissions needed for tribal areas.

Karnataka

Non-agriculturists can buy up to 10 units (1 unit = 1.7 acres in dry land).

Punjab

No restrictions on agricultural land purchase. NRIs need RBI approval.

Our Verification Process

  • Verification of all encumbrances

  • Land ceiling act compliance

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common agricultural land investment queries

NRIs cannot directly purchase agricultural land in India without special permissions under FEMA regulations. However, they can:

  • Inherit agricultural land from relatives
  • Receive agricultural land as a gift from relatives
  • Purchase through specific government schemes with approvals
  • Invest in farmhouses (with land area restrictions varying by state)

We recommend consulting with our legal experts for current regulations based on your specific situation.

Returns vary based on location, crop type, and farming methods:

  • Land appreciation: Typically 8-15% annually in developing areas, with premium locations near infrastructure projects seeing 20-30% during development phases
  • Crop income: 10-25% of land value annually for active farming (varies by crop - horticulture generally yields more than food grains)
  • Lease income: 3-8% of land value annually if leasing to farmers

Our investment calculator can provide personalized projections based on your specific parameters.

Our water verification process includes:

  • Well inspection: Measuring depth, yield and water quality
  • Borewell testing: Flow rate and sustainability assessment
  • Canal access: Verification of irrigation rights and schedules
  • Rainfall data: Analysis of 10-year precipitation patterns
  • Water testing: For salinity, pH and contaminants
  • Neighbor verification: Confirming water availability during summer months

All properties include a detailed water availability report in our due diligence package.

Agricultural land offers several tax advantages:

  • No capital gains tax if held for more than 2 years (considered long-term)
  • Agricultural income exemption up to ₹5,000 per year (unlimited if derived from land cultivation)
  • Deductions for expenses like seeds, fertilizers, labor and equipment
  • State subsidies available for organic farming, drip irrigation and soil conservation
  • Lower property taxes compared to residential/commercial land

Note: Tax benefits vary by state and usage. Consult our tax advisors for personalized advice.
