Healthcare Property FAQs

What makes healthcare real estate different from other commercial properties? +

Healthcare properties have unique requirements including specialized infrastructure (medical gas, backup power), higher parking ratios, strict zoning regulations, and need for proximity to population centers. They also typically have longer lease terms and more stable tenants compared to other commercial real estate.

What are the typical lease terms for medical offices? +

Medical office leases generally run 7-15 years, significantly longer than traditional office spaces. They often include tenant improvement allowances for build-outs and periodic rent escalations tied to CPI or fixed percentages.

How does zoning affect healthcare property development? +

Medical facilities often require specific zoning (M-1, C-2, or healthcare-specific designations) that permits 24/7 operations, higher traffic volumes, and specialized uses. Some municipalities have separate zoning for hospitals, clinics, and diagnostic centers.

What should I look for in a healthcare property investment? +

Key factors include: strong demographics with growing population, proximity to hospitals or other medical providers, flexible floor plans that can accommodate multiple specialties, adequate parking and accessibility, and infrastructure that can support evolving medical technology.

How has COVID-19 impacted healthcare real estate? +

The pandemic accelerated trends toward telehealth, decentralized care delivery, and infection control design. Demand increased for outpatient facilities while highlighting the need for flexible spaces that can adapt to surge capacity requirements.
